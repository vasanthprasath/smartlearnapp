To publish your app so others can download it, you have two main options:Google Play Store: The professional way (Costs $25 one-time fee).1GitHub: The free way (Best for student portfolios/sharing with friends).Since you need a Signed APK (a finalized app file) for both, let's generate that first.Step 1: Generate the Signed App FileYou cannot publish the "Debug" version you use for testing. You need a "Release" version.In Android Studio, go to the top menu: Build 2$\rightarrow$ Generate Signed Bundle / APK.3Select Android App Bundle (.aab) (best for Play Store) or APK (easier for direct sharing). Let's choose APK for now so you can test it.Click Next.Key Store Path: Click Create new...Path: Choose a folder on your computer (like Desktop) and name the file upload-key.jks.Password: Type 123456 (or something you remember).Alias: Write key0.Certificate: Fill in your First/Last Name and "Saveetha" for Organization.Click OK.Enter the password you just created. Click Next.Select Release and check both V1 and V2 boxes.Click Finish.Android Studio will build your app. A popup will appear at the bottom right. Click "Locate" to see your final .apk file.4Option A: Publish on Google Play Store (Professional)Cost: $25 USD (One-time fee)5Go to the Google Play Console.Sign in with your Google account and pay the $25 registration fee.Click Create App.Fill in Details:App Name: SEC Training PortalDefault Language: EnglishApp or Game: AppFree or Paid: FreeDashboard Tasks: You must complete these sections:Privacy Policy: (You can use a free generator online).App Access: Select "All functionality is available without special access".Content Ratings: Answer questions about violence/content (Select "Educational").Target Audience: Select "18 and over".Upload the App:Go to Production on the left menu.Click Create New Release.Upload the Signed .aab file (You might need to repeat Step 1 and choose "Bundle" instead of APK).Store Listing: Upload your App Icon (512x512 px) and Screenshots (take them from your emulator).Click Review and Release. Google typically reviews new apps in 3-7 days.Option B: Publish on GitHub (Free / Student Portfolio)Cost: FreeThis is great for showing recruiters or professors without paying.Go to GitHub.com and create an account.Create a New Repository named SEC-Training-App.Upload your Code:Open your project folder on your computer.Drag and drop the files into GitHub (or use Git commands if you know them).Create a Release (To let people download the App):On your GitHub repository page, look for "Releases" on the right side.6Click Create a new release.Tag version: v1.0.Title: Initial Release.Attach binaries: Drag and drop the Signed APK file you generated in Step 1 here.Click Publish Release.Now you can send the GitHub link to anyone, and they can download the APK file to install on their phone!
